3. Cookies, similar technologies, and advertising

Cookies are small files stored on your device. Similar technologies include local storage, pixels, tags, software development tools, and device or online identifiers.

Essential and security technologies

Our website and infrastructure providers may use technologies that are necessary to deliver the site, maintain security, balance traffic, remember basic settings, and detect malicious or automated activity.

Google AdSense and advertising cookies

When advertising is enabled, third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve, limit, personalize, and measure ads based on a visitor’s prior visits to this website or other websites. Google’s use of advertising cookies enables Google and its partners to serve ads based on visits to this website and/or other websites.

Third parties may place and read cookies on your browser, or use web beacons, IP addresses, and similar technologies in connection with ad serving and measurement. Ads may be personalized or non-personalized depending on your location, settings, and consent choices.

You can learn about and manage Google advertising personalization at Google Ads Settings . You may also find additional industry opt-out choices at YourAdChoices , where available.

Consent and privacy choices

Where required, we use a consent management platform to request and record choices concerning advertising cookies, personalized advertising, analytics storage, and related processing. Visitors may be shown different choices based on their location. A “Privacy and cookie settings” link may also appear on the website so that eligible users can review or change their choices.

Blocking or deleting cookies through your browser may affect certain website functions. Browser controls do not always block all forms of online identification or advertising measurement.
